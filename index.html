1. Rent vs. Buy Simulator

The Rent vs. Buy simulator evaluates housing wealth generation over time. By comparing the cost of renting a property and investing the difference to purchasing a home, it models down payments, home appreciation, annual property tax rates, homeowners insurance, maintenance costs, and renting alternatives.

Primary Equation Model: Buying Costs include: Down Payment + Closing Costs + Monthly Mortgage Payments + Maintenance + Property Taxes + Home Insurance. Renting Costs include: Monthly Rent + Renters Insurance + Opportunity Cost of Down Payment invested in securities.

2. Trucking Cost Per Mile Calculator

Designed for owner-operators and fleet managers, this tool solves for operational efficiency. Enter monthly fixed expenses (insurance, truck payments, permits) and variable operational expenses (fuel, tires, preventative maintenance, driver wages) alongside total miles driven to output your cost-per-mile (CPM) metric.

Primary Equation Model: Total Monthly Costs = Fixed Costs + (Variable Cost per Mile * Miles Driven). Cost Per Mile (CPM) = Total Monthly Costs / Total Miles Driven.

Frequently Asked Questions & Financial Guides

What is a good trucking cost per mile?

A healthy trucking cost per mile (CPM) ranges between $1.50 and $2.20, depending on fuel price, equipment financing rates, maintenance history, and insurance premiums. Keeping detailed logs of fixed and variable expenses allows owner-operators to negotiate profitable freight rates.

How do you compare renting vs buying?

Comparing renting vs buying a home requires looking beyond monthly payments. Buying builds home equity through amortization and asset price appreciation, but incurs non-recoverable costs like property taxes, mortgage interest, and home maintenance. Renting eliminates maintenance risks and allows you to invest capital (like a down payment) into index funds.

What is the benefit of debt consolidation?

Consolidating high-interest credit card debt into a lower-rate personal loan reduces total interest costs and combines multiple monthly payments into a single payment. This helps you pay off the principal balance faster and save money.

How is Google AdSense revenue calculated?

AdSense earnings are calculated by multiplying page impressions by the click-through rate (CTR) and the average cost per click (CPC). For example, 10,000 pageviews with a 2% CTR (200 clicks) at $0.50 CPC generates $100 in ad revenue.
